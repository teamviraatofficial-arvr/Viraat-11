# Weapon Systems: Sniper Rifles & Precision Arms

## DLQ-33 / CheyTac M200 Intervention

The "DLQ-33" is a common designation (often in simulation environments) for the **CheyTac M200 Intervention**, an American bolt-action sniper rifle (gun/firearm) powered by the .408 CheyTac or .375 CheyTac cartridge. It is designed for long-range soft-target interdiction.

### Technical Specifications

- **Caliber**: .408 CheyTac / .375 CheyTac
- **Action**: Bolt-action
- **Effective Range**: 2,500 m+ (2,734 yd)
- **Feed System**: 7-round detachable box magazine
- **Sights**: Day/Night optical sights (Picatinny rail standard)

### Field Assembly & Disassembly Protocol

**WARNING**: Ensure weapon is clear and safe before handling.

#### Assembly Steps:

1.  **Barrel Installation**:
    - Insert the barrel into the receiver extension.
    - Hand-tighten the barrel nut.
    - Use the preset torque wrench to tighten the barrel nut to **75 ft-lbs**.
2.  **Bolt Insertion**:
    - Align the bolt lugs with the receiver raceways.
    - Slide the bolt forward until it locks into battery.
    - Ensure the bolt handle is in the down (locked) position.
3.  **Chassis Attachment**:
    - Place the barreled action into the chassis/stock system.
    - Insert and tighten the action screws (front and rear) to **65 in-lbs**.
4.  **Optic Mounting**:
    - Install the scope rings onto the Picatinny rail.
    - Place the optic into the rings and level the crosshairs.
    - Tighten ring screws in a cross-pattern to **15-20 in-lbs** (do not overtighten).
5.  **Bipod & Muzzle Device**:
    - Attach the bipod to the front rail or spigot mount.
    - Screw on the muzzle brake (note: reverse threads on some M200 variants).

#### Maintenance Check:

- Verify headspace using GO/NO-GO gauges.
- Check trigger pull weight (Standard: 3.5 - 4.5 lbs).
- Lubricate bolt lugs and raceways lightly with CLP.

## M4A1 Carbine

The **M4A1** is a 5.56×45mm NATO, gas-operated, magazine-fed carbine. It is arguably the most recognizable modern military firearm/gun.

### Technical Specifications

- **Caliber**: 5.56×45mm NATO
- **Action**: Gas-operated, rotating bolt (Direct Impingement)
- **Effective Range**: 500 m (point target)
- **Rate of Fire**: 700–950 rounds/min
- **Feed System**: 30-round STANAG magazine

### Field Strip (Disassembly) Protocol

1.  **Clear Weapon**: Remove magazine, pull charging handle, inspect chamber.
2.  **Separate Receivers**: Push out rear takedown pin and front pivot pin.
3.  **Bolt Carrier Group**: Pull charging handle rearward and remove BCG.
4.  **Buffer**: Depress buffer retainer and remove buffer/spring.
5.  **Clean & Lube**: Apply CLP to bolt rings and carrier rails.

## AK-47 / AKM

The **AK-47** is a gas-operated assault rifle chambered in 7.62×39mm. It is known for extreme durability.

### Technical Specifications

- **Caliber**: 7.62×39mm
- **Action**: Gas-operated, rotating bolt (Long Stroke Piston)
- **Effective Range**: 350 m
- **Rate of Fire**: 600 rounds/min

### Field Strip Protocol

1.  **Safety Check**: Remove magazine, rack bolt, verify clear.
2.  **Dust Cover**: Press button on rear of receiver and lift cover.
3.  **Recoil Spring**: Push forward to unseat and remove.
4.  **Bolt Carrier**: Pull carrier to rear, lift out of rails.
5.  **Gas Tube**: Rotate latch on rear sight block and lift tube.
