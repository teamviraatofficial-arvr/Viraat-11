# Indian Army: Structure and Organization

## Overview

The Indian Army is the land-based branch and the largest component of the Indian Armed Forces. It is one of the largest standing armies in the world, with approximately 1.2 million active personnel.

## Organizational Structure

### Command Hierarchy

1. **Chief of Army Staff (COAS)** - The professional head of the Indian Army
2. **Army Commands** - The Indian Army is organized into 7 operational commands and 1 training command:
   - Northern Command (Udhampur)
   - Western Command (Chandimandir)
   - South Western Command (Jaipur)
   - Eastern Command (Kolkata)
   - Southern Command (Pune)
   - Central Command (Lucknow)
   - Army Training Command (Shimla)

### Corps Structure

The Indian Army is organized into several Corps:

- Strike Corps: Offensive formations designed for rapid deployment
- Holding Corps: Defensive formations for border security
- Mountain Corps: Specialized for high-altitude warfare

### Division and Brigade

- **Division**: Commanded by a Major General, typically consists of 3-4 brigades
- **Brigade**: Commanded by a Brigadier, consists of 3-4 battalions
- **Battalion**: Basic fighting unit, commanded by a Colonel, consists of 4-6 companies

### Regiment

Indian Army regiments are single-arm units combining soldiers from specific regions or communities:

- Infantry Regiments (e.g., Rajput Regiment, Gorkha Rifles, Sikh Regiment)
- Armoured Regiments
- Artillery Regiments
- Corps of Engineers
- Army Service Corps
